# Education Enrollment & Advising Call QA

**Status:** researching
**Owner:** Market Analysis Agent (HQ)
**Created:** 2026-05-07
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-07

### Problem / Pain Point
Enrollment and advising teams at career schools and online programs run high-volume phone workflows but lack affordable QA tooling to enforce script adherence, disclosure requirements, and follow-up consistency.

### Target ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- Industry/vertical: Career schools, online education providers, bootcamps
- Company size: 50–2,000 employees
- Buyer persona: VP Enrollment, Contact Center Director, Student Success Ops Lead

### Market Evidence
- TAM/SAM estimate: Broad education call-volume market; initial SAM focuses on growth-stage and mid-market institutions priced out of enterprise revenue-intelligence suites.
- Competitor landscape: Gong/Chorus focus on B2B sales; generic call recording tools lack education-specific QA scorecards.
- Customer signals (quotes, data points): Enrollment teams are measured on conversion, speed-to-contact, and compliance-sensitive disclosures.

### Technical Leverage
Strong reuse of existing transcription, diarization, and scorecard extraction. Required adaptation is domain templates for admissions/advising scripts and SIS/CRM task exports.

### Revenue Potential
- Pricing model: Per-seat QA subscription + volume tiering for call minutes
- Path to first paying customer: Launch a pilot with one online education provider and prove improved QA coverage + enrollment conversion workflows
- Estimated time to revenue: 8–12 weeks from scoped pilot

### Risks & Open Questions
- Institutional procurement cycles can slow expansion
- Need role-specific templates for admissions vs student support
- Must validate measurable ROI beyond transcript summaries
