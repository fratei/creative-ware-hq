# Financial Advisory Call Compliance (RIAs/Broker-Dealers)

**Status:** researching
**Owner:** Market Analysis Agent (HQ)
**Created:** 2026-05-07
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-07

### Problem / Pain Point
Independent RIAs and broker-dealers must review advisor-client conversations for suitability, disclosures, and policy adherence, but existing tooling is either manual sampling or enterprise suites priced for much larger sales organizations.

### Target ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- Industry/vertical: Financial advisory, wealth management, broker-dealer operations
- Company size: 10–250 advisors
- Buyer persona: Chief Compliance Officer, Head of Supervision, COO

### Market Evidence
- TAM/SAM estimate: US independent advisor + broker-dealer supervisory tooling opportunity is sizable within RegTech and conversation intelligence spend; initial AudioText SAM focuses on mid-market firms lacking enterprise budget.
- Competitor landscape: Gong/Chorus are sales-centric and expensive; horizontal transcription APIs require custom compliance workflow buildout.
- Customer signals (quotes, data points): Regulated teams repeatedly need auditable call summaries, risk flags, and supervisory queues rather than generic notes.

### Technical Leverage
High leverage from AudioText transcription, diarization, and post-call extraction. Primary new work is compliance-specific policy templates, exception routing, and export formats for supervisory workflows.

### Revenue Potential
- Pricing model: Per-advisor monthly platform + usage-based overage for call volume
- Path to first paying customer: Pilot with 1–2 independent advisory firms via compliance consultant channel partners
- Estimated time to revenue: 8–12 weeks with template + dashboard MVP

### Risks & Open Questions
- Regulatory interpretation differs by firm and jurisdiction
- Must support auditability and defensible review workflows
- Need clarity on integration priorities (archiving/compliance platforms vs CRM)
