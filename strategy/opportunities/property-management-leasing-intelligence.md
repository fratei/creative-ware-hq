# Property Management Leasing Call Intelligence

**Status:** researching
**Owner:** Market Analysis Agent (HQ)
**Created:** 2026-05-07
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-07

### Problem / Pain Point
Leasing teams lose deals due to inconsistent follow-up and poor capture of resident intent from inbound/outbound calls. Generic meeting-note tools do not provide leasing-specific automation (tour scheduling, objection tagging, and lead-stage updates).

### Target ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- Industry/vertical: Multifamily property management and leasing operations
- Company size: 500–20,000 units under management
- Buyer persona: VP Leasing, Director of Operations, Regional Manager

### Market Evidence
- TAM/SAM estimate: Large multifamily market with recurring leasing-call volume; initial SAM targets mid-market operators using modern PMS/CRM stacks.
- Competitor landscape: Contact center QA tools are broad and expensive; meeting-note products are not tuned to leasing conversion workflows.
- Customer signals (quotes, data points): Operators prioritize occupancy and response-time KPIs, creating direct ROI for call-to-task automation.

### Technical Leverage
Very high reuse of AudioText call transcription + summarization + extraction. Incremental build centers on leasing-specific schemas, scorecards, and PMS/CRM connectors.

### Revenue Potential
- Pricing model: Per-property or per-seat subscription with usage tiers
- Path to first paying customer: Pilot with regional operator managing 2,000+ units and measure lift in tour conversion + follow-up SLA
- Estimated time to revenue: 6–10 weeks for an MVP pilot motion

### Risks & Open Questions
- Integration fragmentation across PMS/CRM systems
- Need robust handling for bilingual leasing calls in key markets
- Adoption depends on manager workflow change, not just transcript quality
